## Design Context

### Users
High school students (Grades 8-12) and their chemistry teachers. Used for classroom assignments, interactive 3D laboratory experiments, and competitive gamified learning (Arena). They need a system that makes learning chemistry engaging rather than intimidating.

### Brand Personality
Gamified, Scientific, Playful. The tone should feel like an engaging educational RPG where chemistry is an adventure ("Odyssey") rather than a chore.

### Aesthetic Direction
Playful, tactile, and slightly retro-futuristic (think clean science lab mixed with modern gaming console UI). 
- Avoid: Generic corporate SaaS looks, boring academic layouts, typical "edtech" minimalism.
- Focus: Tactile elements, bold typography, clear gamified cues, distinct visual hierarchy that guides the student through their learning journey. 
- Theme: Bright, high-contrast light mode with vibrant accent colors (greens, purples, blues) against a clean backdrop.

### Design Principles
1. **Gamification First**: Make interactions feel rewarding and tactile (like pressing game buttons).
2. **Scientific but Approachable**: Use chemistry motifs (atoms, flasks, equations) but stylize them so they aren't dry.
3. **Clear Progression**: The visual hierarchy should pull the user toward their next task or mission.
4. **Bold Contrast**: Use strong, unapologetic color accents against neutral backgrounds to highlight interactive elements.
